# The Evolution of Snapdragon Processor: A Journey Through Time

## Introduction to Snapdragon Processor

The Snapdragon processor has been a cornerstone of modern smartphones, powering some of the most advanced mobile devices in the world. To understand the significance of this processor, it's essential to delve into its history and evolution.

* In 2007, Qualcomm acquired Intel's Mobile Communications Division, marking a significant turning point for the company. This acquisition gave Qualcomm access to Intel's expertise in mobile chip design, setting the stage for the development of the Snapdragon processor.
* The first-generation Snapdragon processor was announced in 2010. Initially, it was based on the Krait architecture and offered improved performance compared to its predecessors. However, this generation also came with limitations, including power consumption and heat dissipation issues.

## Advancements in Snapdragon Processor (2013-2016)

### Introduction of 4G LTE Support

The introduction of 4G LTE support in Snapdragon processors marked a significant milestone in mobile device evolution. This upgrade enabled faster data speeds, lower latency, and improved overall network performance. The adoption of 4G LTE paved the way for the proliferation of mobile broadband services, transforming the way people communicate, work, and access information on-the-go.

### Improved Performance and Power Efficiency

During this period, Qualcomm made substantial improvements to the Adreno GPU, leading to enhanced graphics processing and power efficiency. These advancements enabled smoother gaming experiences, faster app performance, and longer battery life for mobile devices. The improved Adreno GPU also facilitated the development of more complex and visually stunning games.

### Snapdragon 808 Processor (2015) Significance

The Snapdragon 808 processor, announced in February 2015, introduced a new 64-bit CPU architecture, which significantly enhanced performance and efficiency. Notably, it featured a quad-core CPU with four Cortex-A57 cores and six PowerVR G5300 GPU clusters. Although the Snapdragon 808 was not widely adopted due to its high power consumption, it laid the groundwork for future Snapdragon processors.

## Snapdragon 820 and 821: A New Era of Performance

The introduction of the Snapdragon 820 and 821 processors marked a significant milestone in the evolution of mobile processing technology. Released in 2015, these processors brought about several key improvements that set a new standard for performance, power efficiency, and thermal management.

* The Snapdragon 820 and 821 processors were powered by a new Krait CPU architecture, which offered improved performance and power efficiency compared to its predecessors. The Krait architecture also introduced new instructions and features, such as vector floating-point operations (VFPv4) and a more efficient branch predictor.
* In terms of performance, the Snapdragon 820 and 821 processors demonstrated significant improvements over their predecessors. According to Qualcomm's specifications, the Snapdragon 820 processor offered up to 25% better performance than its predecessor, while the Snapdragon 821 processor offered even more impressive gains, with some tasks seeing a 30-40% increase in performance.
* The power efficiency of the Snapdragon 820 and 821 processors was also improved, allowing for longer battery life and reduced heat generation. This was achieved through a combination of architectural changes, such as the use of lower voltage components and improved power management.
* Another notable feature of the Snapdragon 821 processor was its support for 4G LTE-Advanced Pro. This allowed for faster data speeds and improved network performance, making it an attractive option for mobile device manufacturers looking to offer high-speed connectivity to their customers.

Overall, the introduction of the Snapdragon 820 and 821 processors marked a significant step forward in the evolution of mobile processing technology. Their improved performance, power efficiency, and thermal management made them well-suited for a wide range of applications, from gaming to video streaming.

## Snapdragon 835 and 845: Artificial Intelligence and Edge AI

The introduction of artificial intelligence (AI) and edge AI capabilities in the Snapdragon processor marked a significant shift towards intelligent mobile devices. The Qualcomm Neural Engine (QNE), first introduced in the Snapdragon 835, was designed to accelerate machine learning tasks.

*   The QNE significantly improved performance and power efficiency compared to its predecessors, enabling more complex AI applications in mobile devices.
*   With the QNE, developers could integrate AI-powered features into their apps, such as facial recognition, object detection, and natural language processing.
*   The Snapdragon 845 processor took this a step further by supporting Wi-Fi 6 and Gigabit LTE, providing faster data transfer rates and improved connectivity for AI-driven applications.

The introduction of the QNE and the subsequent improvements in the Snapdragon 845 processor enabled a new era of intelligent mobile devices. As AI technology continues to evolve, we can expect to see even more innovative applications in smartphones and other mobile devices.
